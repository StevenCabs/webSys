About Fischer

I. Fischer was titled as a National master at the age of 12, and at the age of 13, he became the youngest person to win the America's Junior Chess Championship.


II. Fischer was invited to participate in the 1957/58 US Championship. He played against titled grandmasters, including the defending US champion Arthure Bisguier. Fischer was able to score 8 wins and five draws, winning the tournament by one point and becoming the youngest US champion at the age of 14.


III. Fischer played a match against Donald Byrne - A strong and renowned chess player. Their match was dubbed the 'Game of the Century' as young Bobby Fischer played very well for his age, and became recognized as a child prodigy and chess genius


IV. At age 15, Fischer became a grandmaster, beating Boris Spassky's record which was 18 years old.


V. Fischer holds the world record of longest winning streak with a score of 20 - 0. During the 1971 chess match for the World Champion Title, he beat elite grandmasters, earning him with perfect scores for each opponents, except for Tigran Petrosian who beat Fischer in a match and ended his streak.


VI. In 1972, Fischer played against Spassky for the World Champion Title. He beat Spassky with a score of 12.5 - 8.5, becoming the 11th World Chess Champion. His victory wa considered as an 'American Victory' against the Soviets who had been dominant in chess for years.


VII. After his win against the Soviets, he made appearances in magazines and TV shows. Because of this, interest in chess has risen. This was commonly referred to as the 'Fischer Boom.'
